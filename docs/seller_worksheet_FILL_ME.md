# Seller Worksheet — FILL ME (free-fill)

How to use: fill the blanks (`___`) for each seller type — type right after the colon. Skip any field
you're unsure of (leave it blank, I'll ask). Short phrases are fine. The first one (Syndicator Exiting)
is **filled in as an example** so you can see the depth — match it or go lighter. When you're done (or
done with what you know), hand it back and I'll turn it into the matrix + wire it into the game,
asking questions only for the gaps.

Fields per seller (all optional):
- **Vibe** — one line on who they are
- **Motivation / Price flex** — High / Med / Low
- **Reply speed** — how many days to respond, and how reachable
- **Tone** — how the buyer has to act with them
- **Decision structure** — solo / partners / heirs / committee
- **Deferred maintenance** — None / Low / Med / High + what kind
- **LOI — gives on / won't move on** — which terms (price, EMD, EMD-hard, DD days, close days, financing contingency, title, extensions, close date)
- **Signature curveball** — the surprise/twist they throw
- **Ripple** — how they affect later steps (DD / closing / asset mgmt)

- Vibe: syndicated ~5 yrs ago, fund clock is up, LPs want their money — "just get me out"

- Tone: businesslike but tired; relationship helps a little
- Decision structure: GP + LP partners — needs partner/LP sign-off on changes
- Deferred maintenance: **High** — under-maintained to juice distributions; roofs, deferred capex, units in rough shape
- LOI — gives on: price (some), DD days, extensions (early) | won't move on: ___
- Signature curveball: hopes you do a light DD and miss the deferred maintenance; if you find it, the **credit renegotiation stalls** while partners debate
- Ripple: DD → big hidden capex only found with 3+ visits or a paid 3rd-party report; AM → that deferred capex hits your Year-1 budget if you missed it
